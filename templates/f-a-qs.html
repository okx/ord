FAQs
What are JTC Ordinals?
JTC Ordinals are a new type of NFT (on non-fungible token) with revolutionary features not available anywhere else. When using JTC Ordinals to mint art NFTs, artists can embed safety features to protect token owners from scams, hacks, and lost private keys. These same protections also support artists’ legal rights, allowing them to recover the token if a buyer violates licensing terms or fails to pay royalties. No other token offers artists these vital capabilities.
What are the benefits of JTC Ordinals for artists?
JTC Ordinals liberate artists from exploitation by NFT marketplaces who sell their works while refusing to collect their royalties. When using JTC Ordinals, artists are empowered to enforce their own rights on-chain, without needing the help of intermediaries like marketplaces.

An additional benefit is that JTC Ordinals incorporate all of the licensing terms the artist requires. This way, the agreement can be viewed on an explorer so there is no dispute or confusion about the artist’s rights. Clear agreements give creators more control over their works and ensures that their rights are respected by others throughout the life of their creations.
How does on-chain enforcement work?
JTC Ordinals are minted on JuratBTC, a unique layer-1 blockchain that connects to courts using a protocol called Jurat. If someone breaks the law or breaches the terms of a legal agreement regarding a digital asset, the judge can issue an order and the JuratBTC blockchain will follow it.

For example, if a hacker steals an NFT, a judge can order it returned to its rightful owner. Likewise, if a buyer fails to pay royalties to the artist, a judge can freeze the NFT until the royalties or paid.

To learn more about Jurat’s court connection technology please visit https://jurat.io/whitepaper/
Can I hold my JTC Ordinals in the same wallet as my JTC coins?
No. Ordinals work by minting data into small units of the blockchain’s native coin. Accordingly, your JTC Ordinals could be mistaken for JTC coins and accidentally sent along with normal transactions. To avoid this, you must create a separate wallet to be used just for your ordinals and keep them in that wallet when minting and trading.
